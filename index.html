About me

I'm currently a Ph.D candidate at Michigan State University pursuing a dual degree between physics and electrical engineering. I enjoy playing board and RPG games with my friends, long–distance cycling tours, writing letters, and generally having a good time. I'm also pretty passionate about science, education, software (and how we build it), and libre culture.

My research

My PhD work has largely focused on using computers to investigate active media—systems with internal degrees of freedom that interact through some radiative process. In particular I've built a software package called QuEST to model the electromagnetic interactions between quantum dots.

Selected publications

  • Collective Rabi dynamics of electromagnetically coupled quantum–dot ensembles. Phys. Rev. A 96, 033816 — Published 8 September 2017
  • Computational dynamics of acoustically driven microsphere systems. Phys. Rev. E 93, 013305 — Published 15 January 2016
